Investment Consistency & GMV Defence

Spend Scaled 43×. ROAS Never Fell Below 10×.

HUL ramped from ₹5.3L at launch to ₹230L+ monthly by October 2025 — a 43× scale-up in six months. Attributed GMV grew in step, peaking at ₹31.97 Cr in November. When spend moderated in Q1 2026, ROAS actually improved to 13–15×, confirming structural incrementality rather than just spend-driven volume.

📈 43× Spend Scale-Up — ROAS Floor Held at 10×
From ₹5.3L in April to ₹230L+ in October — a 43× investment ramp in six months. Through every scale phase, ROAS held above 10× (minimum 10.4× in July) and averaged 13.3× for the full year. Rare scaling discipline for a multi-category portfolio.
🛡️ Q1 2026 Pullback Proved Incrementality
February 2026 spend fell to ₹122.6L (−47% vs October peak) as market cooled. GMV declined proportionally — but ROAS improved to 14.7×. The relationship confirms advertising was generating incremental demand, not recycling organic intent.
🎯 November: Festival Season Fully Captured
November 2025 was peak month: ₹215.6L spend → ₹31.97 Cr attributed GMV at 14.8× ROAS. Nov 1–2 alone generated ₹42.5 Cr in GMV (₹20.8L + ₹21.7L daily). Advertising amplified festive demand rather than just riding it.
Category Portfolio & Brand Defence

From 2 to 7 Categories. Brand, Competition & Generic — All Covered.

HUL launched on April 16, 2025 with just beauty-hygiene and cleaning-household. By January 2026, six more categories were active. Across all of them, campaigns ran three parallel keyword tracks — Brand Search, Competitor Capture and Generic — ensuring no purchase intent on the platform escaped to a rival.

🏆 Health Drinks: Horlicks Owned Every Variant
HFD ran campaigns for 7 Horlicks variants (Standard, Junior, Women's, Chocolate, Diabetes+, Fitness Pro, Lite, Protein) plus Boost RTD — ensuring every health drink shelf query surfaced a HUL product. No variant was left undefended.
⚔️ 3-Track Keyword Strategy Across All Divisions
Every division ran Brand Search (defend own keywords), Competition Search (intercept rival queries), and Generic Search (own the category). This tri-tier coverage ensures HUL captures demand at every stage of purchase intent, not just when shoppers already know the brand.
📡 SOV Above 10% in Core Categories at Launch
In the first week, beauty-hygiene Share of Voice reached 19% and cleaning-household hit 13% — dominant positions established immediately, before competition could react. This front-loaded SOV created the visibility base that subsequent scaling expanded.
