📉 The Decline: Why SPA Alone Wasn't Enough
From October to March, Jyothy Labs operated SPA-only. Spend was cut from ₹12.8L to ₹4.4L as ROAS fell — a rational response, but it created a vicious cycle: less visibility → fewer conversions → lower ROAS → more cuts. By March, the program hit its worst ROAS on record at 2.61×, even as SPA spend recovered to ₹6.6L.
🔄 October Comparison: The Potential That Still Exists
In October 2025, with ₹12.8L SPA spend, the program generated ₹62.8L GMV at 4.9× ROAS. In April 2026, with SPA at ₹8.4L + ₹64.5K display (₹9.1L total), the program generated ₹35.6L at 3.93×. The path back to October-level performance is ≈₹12–13L/month — with display, that number can be reached efficiently.
📈 April: Spend +37%, GMV +106%, ROAS +51%
Compared to March 2026: ad spend rose 37% (₹6.65L → ₹9.06L), but GMV more than doubled (+106%). ROAS improved 51% from 2.61× to 3.93×. This is a non-linear recovery — spend increased linearly but GMV accelerated. Display advertising amplified the SPA investment rather than simply adding to it.
💡 Nov–Dec '25: The Ramp-Down Was a Warning
November saw a 3-day budget gap (Nov 3–5: zero spend) followed by a sharp campaign restructure. December consolidated at ₹29–33K/day SPA. These two months maintained 4.4× ROAS — the last stable baseline before the January–March compression. Display could have prevented the Feb–Mar decline had it been layered in earlier.
April 2026 — Display Impact Analysis

Display Delivered 6.44× ROAS — Best Campaign in the Portfolio.

Exo Noodles Liquid Display launched April 2 at a measured ₹2,930/day. Within two weeks, the combined program was outperforming its pre-launch baseline. SPA ROAS recovered steadily week-over-week while display contributed ₹4.16L in direct attributed GMV on just ₹64.5K spend.

📡 Why Display Works for Exo Noodles Liquid
Dishwash liquid is a high-frequency repurchase category — shoppers return to the category page repeatedly. Display ads on category and product listing pages intercept at-shelf browsing moments, capturing demand before competitor SPA listings appear. The Exo NL Display campaign specifically targets the Noodles Liquid subcategory, Exo's premium and fastest-growing segment.
🚀 The Forward Path: Keep Display On, Scale SPA
The program is now in a healthier state than any point in Q1 2026. With display budgeted at ₹87–90K/month (₹2.9K/day), maintaining it is low-cost. The next lever is scaling SPA back toward ₹10–12L/month: at the current 3.74× SPA ROAS, that would yield ₹37–45L monthly GMV — approaching the October 2025 peak, with display accelerating it further.
